Destination: Mars

Mars is the fourth planet from the Sun and the second-smallest planet in the Solar System. Long the subject of science fiction and human imagination, it is now the frontier of active robotic exploration.

The Red Planet in Focus

A global view of Mars taken by spacecraft, showcasing its distinct reddish-orange hue driven by iron oxide (rust) on its surface.

Key Facts About Mars

If humans are to ever establish a permanent presence on the planet, understanding its unique environmental conditions is vital:

  • Atmosphere: Incredibly thin, composed of over 95% carbon dioxide.
  • Temperature: Extremely cold, averaging roughly -81°F (-62°C).
  • Gravity: Roughly 38% of Earth's gravity—meaning you could jump nearly three times higher.
  • Day Length: A Martian day (called a "sol") is just slightly longer than an Earth day at 24 hours and 39 minutes.
